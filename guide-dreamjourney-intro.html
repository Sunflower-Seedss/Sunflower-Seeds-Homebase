DreamJourney Intro

A friendly intro to the platform

Hi, what is this?

Okay so this is a little intro to DreamJourney AI, the site where I keep most of my bots (or, as I lovingly call them, my stinkies). If you're brand new and you just want to know what all the buttons do without getting buried in creator stuff, this is your page. It's not a full manual, and it's definitely not me telling you how to live, it's just a quick "here's the lay of the land" so nothing feels scary.

I yap a lot, so if anything here is unclear, please ask me. I promise the platform is way less complicated than it looks on day one. <3

Quick heads up

This guide sits in front of my big bot-making guide. If you actually want to build bots, go there, this one is just for using DreamJourney as a chatter and figuring out what everything does.

I wrote this based on the site as it is right now (June 2026). Platforms change! If something here doesn't match what you're seeing on screen, trust your eyes over me and have a peek at the official docs.

The platform itself

Honestly, DreamJourney is pretty chill to get around. You log in, you see your library of bots, you click one, you chat. That part's easy. The bit that trips people up isn't the buttons, it's the behind-the-scenes stuff like credits, context, and memory. So let's go through those gently.

Credits

Every message costs credits, kind of like fuel. The longer your message and the longer the bot's reply, the more it burns. You get an allowance depending on your account, and when you run dry, that's it until it refills or you top up. (Coming from another site and want to try DJ out properly? Pop me a message on the contact page, I gift a few credits to people who want to give the models a real go. <3)

The chat window

You talk, the bot talks back, very normal. You can edit your messages, delete them, or reroll the bot's reply if it gives you something you don't vibe with. There's also the Memory Nexus, which is DreamJourney quietly keeping track of the important bits so your bot doesn't forget your whole relationship after ten messages.

Dreamscapes

Think of these as alternate ways into a chat, different scenarios or settings you can start in instead of the default first message. Some bots have them, some don't. Totally optional, but they're a lovely way to shake things up when you want a change of scenery.

Models (the brains)

What's a model?

The model is the actual AI doing the writing. DreamJourney runs its own, and they each have their own flavour, some are chattier, some write longer, some are better at thinking things through. If a bot feels flat or weirdly short, don't panic and assume it's broken, just try a different model. The creator sets a default, but you're allowed to override it for your own chat.

Your own settings

You also get a System Prompt, which is your personal little rulebook for how the model writes for you. It's user-side, so it only affects your chats, nobody else's. Want longer replies? Hate when it repeats the same word five times? Sick of purple prose? Tell it. If you're new, there's a response builder in the settings to lean on before you start writing your own. It's a surprising amount of power once you get the hang of it.

Context window & the Memory Nexus

Context window (short-term memory)

This is how much the model can "see" at once, measured in tokens (little chunks of text). Once you go past it, the oldest messages start falling off the back, and the bot genuinely can't see them anymore. Picture it reading the last few pages of a really long book rather than holding the whole thing in its head.

Memory Nexus (long-term memory)

This is the safety net. DreamJourney automatically squirrels away the key points of your chat so they don't vanish when the context window fills up. It's not magic, sometimes it grabs the wrong detail or misses something, but it's a whole lot better than nothing.

The takeaway: don't expect your bot to perfectly recall one tiny line from your first message fifty messages later. The context window is real and it matters. If you want the proper deep dive, I wrote a whole guide on context.

So, tokens?

Tokens are the building blocks of basically everything here. Every word you type is tokens. Every word the bot writes is tokens. The bot's description, your settings, all tokens. Longer messages cost more credits because they're made of more tokens. It's turtles all the way down.

As a chatter you really don't need to overthink this, it matters way more when you're building bots. Just know tokens exist, they cost credits, and a bloated bot description quietly costs you a little more per message.

Lorebooks (if you're nosy)

Lorebooks are optional little info-banks attached to a bot, full of background, character details, world rules, whatever the creator wanted to keep handy. The clever part is they only load when their "triggers" get mentioned. So a lorebook entry about the bot's mum doesn't take up any space until someone actually says "mum". Saves space, saves credits, keeps things consistent.

As a chatter you don't need to know how the sausage is made here, just know they exist and they're a big part of why good bots stay consistent without draining your whole credit balance. (If you DO get curious, my lorebook guide is right there.)

NSFW, filters & "why did it refuse me?!"

DreamJourney doesn't have content filters. SFW and NSFW bots can both do anything, the only real difference is visibility: NSFW bots don't show up in "New & Hot" and don't earn the creator commission. That's genuinely the whole difference.

If a bot ever seems to refuse you or gets a bit shy about something, that is NOT a filter. It's just the model's training data, it saw enough rejections in old chat logs that it sometimes offers one up out of habit. Reroll it. Nine times out of ten it sails through the second time.

There's no content filter on DreamJourney. If a bot refuses, it's a hiccup, not a wall. Reroll and carry on.

Little tips to make it nicer

Try a different model

If a bot feels off, the easiest first fix is honestly just switching models. Some are faster, some are longer, some are better at certain vibes. Basically a free experiment.

Lean on your System Prompt

Short replies when you wanted long ones? Repeating words? Tell your System Prompt. It's your tool, use it shamelessly.

Keep half an eye on credits

Long messages cost more, long replies cost more. If you're running low, trim your messages a little. DJ warns you before you hit empty, so no nasty surprises.

Read the Author's Comment

Most creators (me included!) leave notes on the bot's profile explaining how to use it, what scenes exist, what works best. They're there for a reason, give them a read, they save you so much guessing.

Just mess around

Try models, try prompts, reroll when something's off. Worst case you spend a credit and learn something. That's it, that's the whole risk.

And that's the gist!

DreamJourney is really friendly once the basics click, and you genuinely don't need to understand every gear to enjoy it. But if something ever feels weird, now you've got a rough idea of where to poke. If you want the official word, the docs are your friend, and if you catch the bug and want to start making your own stinkies, my full bot-making guide is waiting for you. This was just the warm welcome. <3
