Welcome to A.S.S.

The Arcane Scholarly Society is a hidden university for supernatural beings, tucked beneath Griffith Park in Los Angeles. To humans, it appears as a dull, disused government archive building. To the right kind of person, it reveals itself as a sprawling, architecturally impossible campus of Gothic spires, sentient bookshelves, and a Quad with a dragon fountain. Entrance is free to any supernaturally gifted being aged 18 and up, as long as you’re not human. And definitely not from Lunevale.

Public cover name: Angeles Scholarly Seminary  ,  Mascot: The Fighting Imps  ,  Motto: “Don’t underestimate good A.S.S.”

The Glass Hall
Location

The Glass Hall

A five-story domed space made of enchanted stained glass that reshapes itself depending on the time of day. Hosts most large lectures, theatre performances, and the occasional spontaneous magical duel that everyone pretends wasn’t planned. The stained glass throws shifting patterns of coloured light across everything beneath it — rainbow shapes that move and change as the sun tracks overhead.

Cryptid Commons
Location

Cryptid Commons

A high-ceilinged lounge full of mismatched couches, alchemical snack dispensers, and fireproof beanbags. Bookshelves line the walls alongside warm-toned lamps and odd tables shoved into corners. The unofficial social hub of A.S.S. — the place where study sessions dissolve into gossip, and where everyone eventually ends up at some point in the evening.

The Quad
Location

The Quad

One of the few genuinely calm, stable spaces on campus. A rectangular courtyard between the main academic halls, with flagstone walkways, tidy hedges, and a large circular stone fountain at its centre — a carved dragon with water jetting from its mouth. Students decorate it seasonally and it reliably ends up in scarves come winter. No portals, no dimensional anomalies, no screaming grass. It smells like peppermint in spring, and it’s the first thing new students see when they arrive.

Faculty Tower
Location

Faculty Tower

Shifts location every semester. Students who attempt to enter uninvited may find themselves briefly aged or emotionally challenged. Nobody has figured out the pattern, and nobody’s asking Elm about it directly. Approach with at least one good reason for being there.

Forbidden Wing
Location

The Forbidden Wing

Doesn’t officially exist. Students are definitely not allowed in. There is a sign advertising free Wi-Fi that nobody knows the origin of — it keeps coming back no matter how many times it’s removed. There is probably something evil inside. The administration’s official position is that it is better not to check.

Dorms & Housing
Location

Dorms & Housing

Magically responsive to their occupants — a vampire’s room blacks out at sunrise automatically; a kelpie’s floor turns to shallow water at night. First-years are warned not to slam doors. Some furniture bites when it misses its previous occupant. Admin does not intervene unless the walls start speaking Latin or a student gets swallowed by a closet. Dorms are named Blocks A, B, and C, because there was too much arguing over clever names.

Meet the Characters

Student · Red Fox Demihuman

Beatrice “Trixie” Fairchild

The golden girl of A.S.S., beautiful, rich, popular, and significantly smarter than she lets on. Trixie weaponises warmth with surgical precision, steering conversations exactly where she wants them while leaving people convinced the idea was theirs. Her exam results are immaculate. Her notes are disguised as doodles. She majors in Supernatural Law, and her taste in secret boyfriends runs exclusively to lonely nerds and overlooked misfits.

Principal · Eldritch Entity

Peregrine Elm

The enigmatic, silver-haired head of the Arcane Scholarly Society. Impeccably suited, faintly unsettling, with green eyes that catch the light in ways they probably shouldn’t. He views the university as his grandest experiment and its students as cherished performers in an ongoing production. He founded A.S.S. three centuries ago. The reason his office occasionally smells of void and green flame is not something the administration discusses.
