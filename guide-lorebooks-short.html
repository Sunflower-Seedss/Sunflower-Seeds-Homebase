Lorebook Guide (Short)

DJ Lorebooks: Short Version
← Back to Guides
A condensed reference version. For the full guide with examples and in-depth explanations, see the full lorebook guide. This is specifically for DreamJourney AI, lorebook structures are not universal.

Hard Limits

  • Lorebook content is pulled by the model, the entire lorebook is not “read” like a document.
  • A maximum of 1500 tokens of lorebook content can be loaded at once. Past 1500, it won’t be included.
  • Trigger logic cannot be changed.
  • Both your messages and the bot’s can trigger lorebook entries.
If you understand the terms already and just want cascade solutions, skip to section 8.
1

Tokens, What They Actually Are

A token is not a word. It is a chunk of text the model processes. Punctuation, formatting, and whitespace also count.

  • 1 token ≈ 0.75 English words / 4 characters.
  • 1000 tokens ≈ 700–800 words.
  • Long, dense prose eats tokens fast.

The model can only load 1500 tokens of lorebook entries at once. If triggered entries exceed that, it cuts at 1500, and you are not told which entries were dropped.

2

Triggers, How Lore Gets Pulled In

Lorebook entries don’t exist within the chat unless triggered. A trigger tells the model: “Include this entry now.”

  • Triggers are literal. “Dave’s car” does not trigger on “Dave” or “car” alone.
  • Pluralization and spelling matter. Capitalisation does not.
  • Punctuation can be used to prevent accidental triggering.

Critical entries need simple, unavoidable triggers. Non-essential entries need tight triggers so they don’t eat your token budget when irrelevant.

3

Context Window

Everything the model can actively see and reason over: persona and details, user persona, system prompt, Nexus history, recent messages, triggered lorebook entries (up to 1500 tokens), and the author’s note.

When the window is full, older or lower-priority information is pushed out. The model doesn’t decide what’s important, it just loses whatever doesn’t fit. Lorebook entries compete with everything else in the window.

4

“Lost in the Middle”

Models pay more attention to the beginning and end of what they read. Middle content is more likely to be ignored.

  • Put critical facts at the top of each entry.
  • Prefer more smaller entries over fewer large ones.
5

Cascade, Why One Trigger Pulls Too Much

Cascade happens when triggering one entry causes other entries to trigger. One name can pull a bio, which mentions a location, which pulls a location entry, which mentions a faction, and so on.

If those entries together exceed 1500 tokens, the last entries are dropped. The model will invent content to fill the gap, causing characters to be misgendered, features changed, or behaviour contradicted.

Ways to limit cascade

  • Avoid cross-referencing everything everywhere.
  • Keep entries narrow in scope.
  • Do not repeat full lore in multiple places.
  • Wrap trigger words inside entries using punctuation: /trigger/ or _word_.
6

What You Can and Cannot Do

Cannot: increase the 1500 token limit, change how triggers are evaluated, control which entries are dropped, or force the model to remember unused lore.

Can: shorten entries, tighten language, redesign triggers, and wrap trigger words to stop cascade.

Lorebooks are conditional memory injections, not an unlimited encyclopedia. They’re only loaded when triggered.
7

Basic Rules That Help

  • Critical facts go first.
  • No decorative prose.
  • Triggers must be stupid-simple.
  • If it must never be forgotten, it must be short.
  • If everything is important, nothing is.

If the model ignored your lore, the answer is almost always: it never triggered, it triggered but was dropped, or it was loaded but buried in the middle.

8 & 9

How To Reference Without Cascading

Wrap trigger words with punctuation. The model understands the meaning but the trigger doesn’t fire:

Bob drives his _car_to_work_ every day.

Using _ negates the “car” and “work” triggers. The References section in the lorebook UI will show as empty once wrapping is working correctly.

Cascade is fine when it’s two or three small entries unlikely to load alongside other entries. Think about when the cascade will realistically occur and whether the combined token count stays under 1500.
